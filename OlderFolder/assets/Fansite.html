Why I enjoy listening to Periphery


Bio

Periphery is a Djent and Progressive metal bgand based in Washington DC. What began as the side project of founding member Misha Mansoor's guitar instructional YouTube channel has become one of the most technically pollished, musically expressive, hardest working acts currently performing. Their unique sonic fingerprint tangibly contributes to the genre, and their abilityh to accurately reproduce their studio recordings in a live setting is uncanny and unmatched. Their sound is in part defined by intricate rhythms at faster tempos in mixed-meter or polymetric structures. This creates a disjunct character in the strings. The drums knit the seperate meters together, as long note-value melodies soar over this unstable ground. Each member of the group is a master of the techniques associated with multiple genres of music on their respective instruments allowing them to bring material to their output that is as surprising as it is satisfying.


The music is a complex, challanging, aquired taste, though well worth the effort spent learning to hear it. When one learns to listen to Jazz for instance, it requires them ot wrap their ear around unfamiliar hamonies and rhythms. The dissonances, the long distances between arival points, and the rhythmic complexity sound jarring at first. Periphery's music is no different. In a culture where music has become a pleasantly ignorable noise-cloud providimg a sound track to driving, exercise or socializing, the presence of musicians who will not compromise their vision by spoon-feeding their audience is refreshing; and the continuation of new musics designed specifically to be listened to attentively is necesary.

Features of Periphery's Music

  1. Rich, complex chord voicings with shifting exetensions atypical for the genre.
  2. Diverse sound sets, most commonly associated with (but not limited to) Djent and Prog.
  3. Various lengths of work.
  4. Relatively intricate forms in the longer works.
  5. Absolute technical master.
  6. Along the lines of most prog their albums function cohesively as large scale works, bound together by relating structural elements to recurrent thematic material.
  7. The lyrical content is often playful, abstract, or focused on somekind of social justice issue.
  8. Use of electronics, programming and extended instrumental techniques.

Follow the link below to hear a fantastic introduction to their music, a somewhat more excessable work from their latest album from April of 2019. Listening for these few things will add value to the experience and help unlock their idiom in your inner ear:

  1. The syncopation at the beginning makes it tricky to determine the number of beats per bar, but you can still follow the pulse easily.
  2. Try to remember this first theme, and notice when it returns how new themes are layered above and below it.
  3. Listen for a pattern of activity level defined by a gradual increase in activity of guitars and drums, contrasted with a concurent lengthening of the note-values in the vocals punctuated with small ornaments.

Garden in the Bones
A challenge to the listener

One of the things that makes them distinct in a popular genre is the use of counterpoint to achieve the typical palmmutted sound of guitar chords in metal. They make use of their three guitarist to build up the texutre of these chords from single-line melodies that result in harmonies as they line up together. One of the most interesting features of re-listening to their music is hearing this change over time. At first you hear what you think are chords. Upon several subsiquint listens the melodic lines that are happening at the same time reveal themselves. At first, this is may be easier to see than it is to hear. The linked video below demonstrates this effect. Learning how to listent to this music will reward you with this experience of suddenly emerging melodic lines, and other similar surprises.
